What is Escrow?

Understanding Escrow

Escrow is a financial arrangement where a third party holds and regulates payment of funds for two parties in a transaction. It helps make transactions more secure by keeping the payment in a secure escrow account until all terms of an agreement are met.

How Escrow Works

Step 1: Agreement

Buyer and seller agree on terms, and the buyer initiates an escrow transaction on our platform.

Step 2: Secure Payment

Buyer sends funds to Swift Escrow's secure wallet, where they are held safely.

Step 3: Verification

Seller delivers goods or services to the buyer as agreed.

Step 4: Confirmation

Buyer confirms receipt and satisfaction with the goods or services.

Step 5: Release of Funds

Swift Escrow releases the funds to the seller, completing the transaction securely.

Benefits of Using Escrow

For Buyers:

  • Protection from fraud
  • Verification of goods/services before payment
  • Secure payment handling
  • Professional dispute resolution

For Sellers:

  • Guarantee of payment for delivered goods/services
  • Protection from payment reversals
  • Increased buyer confidence
  • Professional transaction management
